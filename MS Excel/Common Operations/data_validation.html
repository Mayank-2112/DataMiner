Data Validation

Data validation in Microsoft Excel is a feature that allows you to control the type and quality of data entered into a cell or a range of cells. It helps ensure that the data entered meets specific criteria, such as a specific format, a range of values, or a custom formula.

Steps To Follow

Here's how you can use data validation in Excel:

Step 1:
Select the cell or range of cells where you want to apply data validation.

Step 2:
Go to the "Data" tab in the Excel ribbon.

Step 3:
Click on the "Data Validation" button in the "Data Tools" group. This will open the "Data Validation" dialog box.

Step 4:
In the "Settings" tab of the dialog box, you can choose the type of data validation you want to apply.
To allow only specific values, select "List" from the "Allow" drop-down menu. Then enter the allowed values in the "Source" field, separated by commas.
To specify a range of numeric values, select "Whole number" or "Decimal" from the "Allow" drop-down menu. Then enter the minimum and maximum values in the "Minimum" and "Maximum" fields.
To enforce a specific text length, select "Text length" from the "Allow" drop-down menu. Then enter the minimum and maximum text lengths in the "Minimum" and "Maximum" fields.
You can also use other options like date, time, custom formulas, etc., depending on your specific validation requirements.

Step 5:
In the "Input Message" tab, you can enter a message that will be displayed when the cell is selected, providing instructions or guidance for entering data.

Step 6:
In the "Error Alert" tab, you can specify the error message to be displayed if the entered data violates the validation rules. You can choose from options like "Stop," "Warning," or "Information" to control the user's response to the error.

Step 7:
Click "OK" to apply the data validation to the selected cell(s).
